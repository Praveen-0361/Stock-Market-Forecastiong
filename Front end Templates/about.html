ABOUT PROJECT

"This project explores the forecasting of stock prices using various advanced machine learning and statistical techniques. The dataset utilized consists of 2,417 records of historical stock market data, including features such as Adj Close, Open, High, Low, Close prices, and Volume. Each feature is recorded with a float64 precision for price-related attributes and int64 precision for trading volume"

The core focus of the project is to evaluate the performance of several predictive models, specifically Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, Stacked LSTM networks, Gated Recurrent Units (GRU), Stacked GRU networks, and the Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) model. LSTM and GRU are types of Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) that are particularly effective in handling time-series data due to their ability to capture long-term dependencies. Stacked versions of these networks aim to improve performance by increasing the depth of the model, which can help in learning more complex patterns from the data. The dataset encompasses essential stock metrics such as opening, closing, highest, and lowest prices along with trading volume. By applying these diverse forecasting techniques, the project aims to determine which model offers the most accurate predictions for future stock prices. The comparison of these methods highlights their strengths and limitations, providing insights into their suitability for financial forecasting tasks. This evaluation is critical for developing robust models capable of guiding investment strategies and decision-making in stock market contexts.
